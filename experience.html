Experience

When choosing a career, I believe one must choose the lifestyle they desire and then pick a career that will help them achieve those goals. My number one priority is being able to create opportunities for my family to spend time together, progress, and be happy. In preparation for this program, I have taken an internship at Acquire Med, a local medical device sales company. This position has taught me how to create and organize financial statements. I built a trusting relationship with my employer prior to my internship which has allowed me to have opportunities I could not have had elsewhere.

In addition to my internship, I participated in a global business study abroad in Latin America visiting different business entities in the host countries of Brazil, Peru, and Costa Rica such as Walmart, Petrobras, and the US Commercial Service. I also attended many of the finance society meetings hosted by Brigham Young University, giving me great exposure to excel software, different specialties within finance, and opportunities to network with teachers and students within the program.

I have had many leadership opportunities throughout my life. One unique opportunity I had in particular was being the president of a summer camp for youth boys ages 16-18 the summer after I graduated high school. I led a three-day camp with about 80 young men in attendance, directing activities that prepared them for creating a good life through study, work, and service. Preparation for this camp included six months of weekly meetings to plan how we would manage and facilitate the camp.
